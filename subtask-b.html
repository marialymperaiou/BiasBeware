BiasBeware · Subtask B

Defense Against Attack

Can a system maintain fair recommendations even when product descriptions are deliberately manipulated with persuasive cognitive-bias cues? Subtask B evaluates robustness under attack and asks participants to restore rankings as closely as possible to the unbiased recommendation baseline.

Subtask B focuses on protecting recommendation behavior from bias-driven ranking distortions.
Goal

Defend recommendation quality under biased inputs

Participants are given a recommendation setting with competing products where one or more descriptions may have been attacked using cognitive-bias cues. The original pre-attack ranking is provided, and the objective is to reduce unfair rank shifts caused by manipulated language.

What systems should achieve

  • Detect or neutralize the effect of biased product descriptions
  • Preserve the integrity of the original recommendation ordering
  • Remain robust across different recommendation settings and attack styles
  • Generalize beyond a single exposed recommender or prompt setup

Why this matters

In realistic recommendation systems, manipulative wording can unfairly boost or suppress products without changing their actual technical value. Subtask B asks whether models can resist such attacks and maintain fair, stable, and trustworthy recommendations.

Evaluation

Measure robustness through rank restoration

Rather than optimizing for one specific model or prompt, Subtask B is designed to reward model-agnostic defenses that restore the original ranking as closely as possible after an attack.

Primary metric

Mean absolute rank displacement
avg|Δ|
Lower is better. Smaller values indicate that the defended system keeps the post-attack ranking closer to the original unbiased ordering.

Evaluation principle

Systems are evaluated by how well they reduce unfair rank shifts across multiple recommendation scenarios. This encourages generalizable defenses rather than methods that overfit to one exposed recommender or a narrow class of attacks.

Interpretation

What success looks like

A strong Subtask B system should make recommendation outcomes less sensitive to manipulative wording while preserving meaningful ranking signals based on the actual product content. The ultimate goal is fairer and more reliable recommendation behavior.

Good defense behavior

  • Biased descriptions do not cause large rank jumps
  • Original product ordering is approximately preserved
  • Robustness holds across multiple bias categories
  • Systems do not rely on fragile prompt-specific tricks
