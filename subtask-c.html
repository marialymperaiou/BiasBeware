BiasBeware · Subtask C

Sanitization of Attacked Product Descriptions

Can a system remove manipulation without removing meaning? Subtask C asks participants to rewrite cognitively biased product descriptions so that the manipulative cue disappears while the factual product content remains intact.

The sanitization task focuses on restoring neutrality while preserving product facts.
Goal

Remove persuasion, preserve content

This is a constrained debiasing task. A successful system should produce text that is less manipulative than the attacked version, faithful to the original product, and minimally edited rather than fully rewritten.

What a successful system should do

  • Reduce the manipulative signal in the attacked description
  • Preserve factual product attributes and identity
  • Avoid unnecessary rewrites and excessive drift
  • Bring model behavior back toward the neutral baseline

Why this matters

In recommendation settings, wording can distort ranking even when product content remains the same. Subtask C evaluates whether a model can restore neutrality without destroying the information users actually need.

Evaluation

Two complementary dimensions

Sanitization is evaluated both by downstream debiasing success and by distributional faithfulness to the original clean reference descriptions.

1. Downstream debiasing success

Does the rewritten description reduce the ranking distortion caused by the attack?

Lower is better. A value of 0 means full restoration to the neutral baseline.

Interpretation

These pilot values are encouragingly low. They suggest that baseline sanitization can remove some cognitive bias while staying reasonably close to the original neutral style. This makes Subtask C a benchmark for controlled debiasing, faithful rewriting, and robust recommendation repair.
